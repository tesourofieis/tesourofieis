Homily by St Jerome, Priest (at Bethlehem.)
!Bk. i. Comment, on Matth. ix.
The other Evangelists, out of tenderness towards the reputation and honour of~
Matthew, have abstained from speaking of him as a publican by his ordinary name,~
and have called him Levi. Both names were his. But Matthew himself according to~
that Solomon hath The just man is the first to accuse himself, (Prov. xviii.~
17), and again, in another place Declare thou thy sins that thou mayest be~
justified, doth plainly call himself Matthew the publican, to show unto his~
readers that none need be hopeless of salvation if he will but strive to do~
better, since he himself had been all of a sudden changed from a publican into~
an Apostle.
